Aperture: F1.4, F5.6, F8, ...

An Aperture is the opening through which light passes like iris.

The lower f/stops give more exposure because they represent the larger apertures, while the higher f/stops give less exposure because they represent smaller apertures.

At the f/stops, depth is important.

Depth is the zone of acceptable sharpness in front of and behind the subject on which the lens is focused, in other words, is out focusing.

When you choose the higher the f/stop (ex. F6.3), the opening of lens is smaller, the picture is more sharper.

And, when you choose the lower the f/stop (ex. F1.4), the opening of lens is bigger, the picture is blurry except focus.
